Era of technology

Today there are technologies everywhere. Humans depend upon the technologies for almost everything from brushing their teeth to using bathrooms. Nowadays almost every human have at least one of the many technologies. For an instance, let’s take a daily life schedule of an average american man. First thing in the morning, he shuts his alarming clock which is one of the analog technology. Then he brushes his tooth by an electric toothbrush. And uses his mobile phone almost once in every hour. Furthermore, living these daily activities behind, we can take banks, hospitals, offices and military into account as well. Because these places use great technologies for working. Computers are the greatest advancement of human kinds. And the history of computer is very long. First computer was ENIAC made by John Presper Eckert & John Mauchly. Since then computers have been advancing day by day. Later on many developers like Charles Babbage developed many computers which have been great help since then. Impact On Our Lives

Demand for new and evolving technologies has increased, putting more strain on the planet and its natural resources. Various numbers of computers and mobile phones are produced regularly. If the current epoch continues, our future generation will be confronted with several issues. Various health facilities have been improved as a result of the widespread use of technology by delivering health technologies that either directly or indirectly affect human lives. This has a positive impact on developed countries, but it harms developing countries because they are unable to access the healthcare benefits provided by the technology. Population growth in developed countries is controlled by a variety of advanced birth control methods that allow for population balance in terms of natural resources and other benefits associated with planned population. The mindset of developing countries, on the other hand, is very different. The birth and death rates are both extremely high, but there is a scarcity of food and poor health.

Ways To Limit Use Of Technology

An entire day without technology (or a morning off if you're a beginner) allows you to rest, relax, and take in new opportunities offline. Take time to read a physical book, make something with your hands, garden, or sit and reflect on life before technology took over – be grateful that we are old enough to remember life before the ubiquitous machine.When driving or taking a walk in the woods, put your phone on silent mode. When you're out shopping, leave it in your car. Consider what it's like to be a kid again, with just a landline and a promise to be somewhere on time – and actually turn up? Isn't it freeing not to be tethered to a daily reminder that we're still needed right now?
